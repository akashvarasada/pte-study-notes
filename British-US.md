# British vs US Convention

## PTE Tips and Tricks

| Pattern | British English | US English | How to identify |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| -our / -or | Colour, Favourite, Honour | Color, Favorite, Honor | British often keeps **u** in **-our** words. US usually removes **u** and uses **-or**. |
| -ise / -ize | Organise, Recognise, Realise | Organize, Recognize, Realize | British commonly uses **-ise**. US commonly uses **-ize**. |
| -re / -er | Centre, Theatre, Metre | Center, Theater, Meter | British often ends with **-re**. US usually reverses it to **-er**. |
| Double consonant / single consonant | Traveller, Cancelled, Labelled | Traveler, Canceled, Labeled | British often doubles **l** before endings. US often uses one **l**. |
| -ce / -se | Defence, Offence, Licence | Defense, Offense, License | British often uses **-ce** for nouns. US often uses **-se**. |
| -ogue / -og | Catalogue, Dialogue, Analogue | Catalog, Dialog, Analog | British often keeps **-ogue**. US often shortens it to **-og**. |
| Word choice | Flat, Lift, Holiday | Apartment, Elevator, Vacation | Some differences are not spelling changes; they are different words. |
| PTE consistency | British or US | British or US | In PTE Writing, choose one style and stay consistent throughout the answer. |
| Reading strategy | Look for spelling patterns | Look for spelling patterns | In Fill in the Blanks, spelling style can help you eliminate options quickly. |
